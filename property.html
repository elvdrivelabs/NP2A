Solution for dispute resolutions

While a dispute may arise from more factors than those listed here, being aware of these common disputes is helpful :

Title Disputes

Title disputes can arise when multiple parties claim ownership of the same property often caused by errors in the land records, boundaries, or fraudulent conveyances.

Encroachments

Encroachments occur when a neighbouring property owner uses or builds on a portion of another person’s land which can cause property damage, disputes over boundaries, and legal liability.

Easements

Easements are rights granted to a party to use a portion of another person’s land for a specific purpose. These often cause disputes over the scope of the easement, the right to access the easement, or the maintenance of the easement.

Zonning Violations

Zoning violations occur when a property owner uses their property in a way that violates local zoning laws or regulations. This can result in fines, legal action, or the loss of the property’s value.

Environmental Issues

Environmental issues can arise when a property is contaminated or has other environmental hazards that impact its value or use. Such cases often result in legal liability, clean-up costs, and difficulty selling the property.
